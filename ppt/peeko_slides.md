# Slide 6: How It Works

## HEADLINE
From Audio to Recovery in Seconds

## SUBHEADLINE
A simple flow that runs silently while you focus on the lecture.

## BULLETS
- Mic captures lecture audio live
- AI generates summary cards every 5 min
- Tap "Catch Me Up" anytime to rejoin
- Peeko PiP shows keywords on any screen

## PRESENTER NOTES
Here's the flow. You start a session and Peeko listens through your mic. Every five minutes, Claude reads the latest transcript and generates a summary card — title, key bullets, keywords. These stack up into a live timeline on your sidebar. Whenever you zone back in, you tap Catch Me Up and get a structured guide: here's what's happening now, here's what you missed, read these cards, here's the quick context to follow along. Meanwhile, Peeko — our little fox — floats in a Picture-in-Picture window showing keyword bubbles from the latest card. Even if you're on YouTube, you see the lecture's pulse.

## VISUAL DIRECTION
A clean horizontal flow diagram moving left to right: microphone icon → audio waves → "AI" processing node → summary card icon → timeline stack. Below or branching off: a "Catch Me Up" button leading to a structured response preview, and a small PiP window with the fox icon. Use soft connecting arrows or lines. Light background, warm accent colors for the nodes. The diagram should feel effortless and elegant — not technical. Think product marketing, not architecture diagram.

# Slide 9: Why This, Why Now

## HEADLINE
Built on Tech That Just Became Possible

## SUBHEADLINE
Three recent breakthroughs converge to make Peeko real.

## BULLETS
- Claude API: structured summaries in real time
- Deepgram: streaming speech-to-text, low latency
- Document PiP API: floating windows, Chrome 116+
- This stack didn't exist two years ago

## PRESENTER NOTES
People might ask — why hasn't someone built this before? Because they couldn't. Claude's ability to return structured JSON summaries from raw transcript in real time — that's new. Deepgram's streaming speech-to-text with low enough latency to feel live — that's new. The Document Picture-in-Picture API that lets us float a window above any app — Chrome only shipped that in 2023. This combination of AI, streaming audio, and browser APIs is what makes Peeko possible right now. We didn't just use new tech for the sake of it. We waited until the stack could deliver the experience students actually need.

Each card has the tech name and a one-line label. Below or connecting them: a convergence point that leads to the Peeko logo. Use a clean, slightly technical aesthetic — dark or navy background with glowing accent lines connecting the three pillars to a central point.
